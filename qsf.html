1. When does the Research Ethics Review Board (RERB) meet?

The CGHMEC RERB meets via Zoom or face-to-face every 3RD or 4TH Thursday of the month and releases its approval/query letter 1 week after the said RERB meeting. Turnaround time from submission to approval is usually around 2 to 3 months.

2. When is the cut-off of submission for initial review and post-approval review?

(Amendment, continuing review, protocol deviation, early termination, final report, SAE, notification)

3. How many copies of the protocol should I submit?

Only the original copy is required. Additional copies may be requested depending on the specific protocol.

4. Is the Principal Investigator (PI) required to be present at the board meeting where the protocol will be discussed?

Yes, the Principal Investigator (PI) is required to be present at the meeting where the protocol will be discussed unless stated otherwise.

5. How long is the validity of the ethical clearance?

The ethical clearance is valid for 1 year from the date of approval unless otherwise specified by the Research Ethics Review Board.

6. When do we pay the ethics review fee?

The ethics review fee is paid upon submission of the protocol for review, before the review process begins.

7. Who prepares the CTA?

The Clinical Trial Agreement (CTA) is typically prepared by the institution's legal team or the Research Ethics Board in coordination with the research sponsor.
